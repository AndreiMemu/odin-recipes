Pan-Seared Duck with Berry Compote & Apple-Kale Slaw

Description

This dish is a sophisticated and romantic pairing that highlights the rich, gamey depth of the perfectly seared duck. The star is the duck breast itself, cooked until the skin is intensely crispy and golden while the meat remains tender and flavorful. It is elegantly balanced by a sweet and tangy berry apple compote, which cuts through the richness of the meat. Served alongside a fresh Kale and Apple Salad, the meal offers a formidable combination of warm, savory poultry and bright, fruity acidity.

Ingredients:

  • The Duck & Prep

    • 2 boneless duck breasts (approx. 6 oz each)
    • Kosher Salt & Black Pepper

  • The Cherry Reduction

    • 1 cup fresh or frozen cherries (pitted and halved)
    • ½ cup red wine (Pinot Noir or Merlot)
    • ½ cup chicken stock
    • 1 tbsp balsamic vinegar & 1 tbsp honey
    • 1 shallot & 2 cloves garlic (minced)

  • The Sides & Finish

    • 1 lb parsnips (peeled/chopped) & 1 lb baby carrots
    • ½ cup heavy cream & ¼ cup milk
    • 2 tbsp cold unsalted butter (for the sauce)

Steps:

  1. Prepare the Elegant Sides

    1. Parsnip Purée: Boil parsnips and garlic until tender (15-20 min). Drain and blend with cream, milk, and butter until completely smooth.
    2. Honey Carrots: Toss carrots with honey and oil. Roast at 425°F for 20 minutes until tender and caramelized.

  2. Render and Sear the Duck

    • Scoring: Use a sharp knife to score a crosshatch pattern into the duck skin. Be careful not to cut into the meat.
    • Searing: Place skin-side down in a cold skillet. Turn to medium heat and render the fat for 8-10 minutes until the skin is very crispy. Flip and sear the meat side for 2-4 minutes.

  3. The Cherry Reduction Phase

    • Deglazing: Sauté shallots and garlic in the duck pan. Add cherries, wine, stock, balsamic, and honey. Simmer until the liquid reduces by half.
    • Finishing: Remove from heat and whisk in the cold butter until the sauce is glossy and thick.

  4. Rest and Serve

    • Resting: Let the duck rest on a cutting board for 5-10 minutes before slicing to keep it juicy.
    • plating: Slice the duck on a diagonal and serve over the parsnip purée, garnished with the glazed carrots and a drizzle of cherry sauce.
